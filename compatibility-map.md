## DRIVE MODE PAIRINGS

### Builder + Builder — "The Force Field"
When aligned: unstoppable. Two people who move fast, execute well, and don't need to be managed.
When misaligned: power struggle. Both want the wheel. Neither naturally holds the space.
The friction point: who leads when you both lead?
The growth edge: learning to hand the wheel without losing yourself.

### Builder + Amplifier — "The Engine Room"
Builder executes. Amplifier energizes. One moves, one motivates.
Natural rhythm: Amplifier rallies, Builder delivers.
The friction point: Builder wants results; Amplifier wants connection along the way.
The growth edge: Builder learns to celebrate the journey. Amplifier learns that momentum is its own motivation.

### Builder + Anchor — "The Foundation"
The most naturally complementary Drive pairing.
Builder drives forward. Anchor holds the ground.
Neither steps on the other's role. Classic lead/hold dynamic.
The friction point: Builder's pace can feel destabilizing to Anchor. Anchor's steadiness can feel like resistance to Builder.
The growth edge: Builder learns that staying is also building. Anchor learns that moving is also safety.

### Builder + Architect — "The Blueprint"
Architect thinks it. Builder builds it.
Together they are strategy + execution — a powerful combination.
The friction point: Architect wants to plan longer. Builder wants to move now.
The growth edge: Builder learns that thinking is not stalling. Architect learns that done is better than perfect.

### Amplifier + Amplifier — "The Spark"
Electric. Exciting. Full of possibility and momentum.
The risk: who grounds the energy? Two high-energy people can burn bright and burn out.
The friction point: neither is wired to hold the space or think three steps ahead.
The growth edge: learning to take turns carrying the vision.

### Amplifier + Anchor — "The Current"
Amplifier brings the energy. Anchor keeps it steady.
Anchor gives Amplifier a home base. Amplifier gives Anchor a reason to move.
The friction point: Amplifier may feel Anchor is holding them back. Anchor may feel Amplifier is never still.
The growth edge: Amplifier learns that steadiness is not limitation. Anchor learns that energy is not chaos.

### Amplifier + Architect — "The Vision"
Amplifier sells it. Architect maps it.
Together: the vision exists and it has a plan.
The friction point: neither is naturally the executor. The dream can stay a dream.
The growth edge: learning to bring in action — or growing that muscle in each other.

### Anchor + Anchor — "The Still Water"
Deep peace. Reliability. A relationship where both people feel safe.
The risk: who drives them forward? Two Anchors can create a life that is comfortable but static.
The friction point: growth requires movement. Neither is wired to initiate.
The growth edge: learning that stability and growth are not opposites.

### Anchor + Architect — "The Long Game"
Both patient. Both thoughtful. Neither rushes.
Architect plans. Anchor holds the space for the plan to unfold.
The friction point: both can be slow to act — Anchor waits, Architect thinks. Things can stall.
The growth edge: learning that forward is also safe.

### Architect + Architect — "The Blueprint Battle"
Two people who both believe they have the right plan.
When aligned: extraordinary foresight and intentionality.
When misaligned: competing systems. Analysis paralysis.
The friction point: whose plan wins?
The growth edge: learning to build one map together instead of two separate ones.

## AMOR CODE PAIRINGS

### Expressive + Expressive — "The Conversation"
Words flow in both directions. Rich verbal intimacy.
Both affirm, both acknowledge, both need to be told.
The friction point: in conflict, words can become weapons. Both are sensitive to what is said and how.
The growth edge: learning that sometimes silence is also connection.

### Expressive + Devoted — "The Classic"
One says it. One shows it. Highly complementary.
Devoted acts without needing to be asked. Expressive tells without needing proof.
The gap: Devoted may feel unacknowledged — they do so much but rarely hear it. Expressive may feel love isn't proven in action.
The growth edge: Devoted learns to say it. Expressive learns to see it in the doing.

### Expressive + Intentional — "The Deep Conversation"
Expressive gives words. Intentional gives time.
When both are present, the connection is rich and layered.
The gap: Intentional may feel Expressive doesn't slow down enough. Expressive may feel time alone isn't enough without acknowledgment.
The growth edge: Expressive learns to be still. Intentional learns to speak into the stillness.

### Expressive + Present — "The Voice and the Body"
Words meet touch. Two different registers of love.
Complementary when both are fluent in what the other offers.
The gap: Expressive may feel Present doesn't say it enough. Present may feel words are hollow without physical closeness.
The growth edge: learning each other's language instead of waiting for the other to speak theirs.

### Expressive + Intimate — "The Open Book"
Both go deep — but differently.
Expressive speaks to connect. Intimate goes deep to be known.
Highest potential for profound intimacy when both lean in.
The gap: Intimate may feel Expressive talks a lot but doesn't always go where it's real. Expressive may feel Intimate withholds.
The growth edge: Expressive learns the difference between talking and opening. Intimate learns that words can carry real depth.

### Devoted + Devoted — "The Team"
Both show love through action. The relationship runs.
Practical, reliable, mutual.
The risk: neither may ask for what they need emotionally. Both keep serving without ever being served the way they need.
The growth edge: learning to stop doing long enough to say what's true.

### Devoted + Intentional — "The Caretaker"
Devoted serves. Intentional is present.
The gap: Devoted may feel time isn't being made for them. Intentional may feel Devoted is always doing instead of just being.
The friction: one gives through action, one gives through presence — can feel like they keep missing each other.
The growth edge: Devoted learns to stop and sit. Intentional learns that showing up in action is also love.

### Devoted + Present — "The Steady Warm"
Service meets touch. Both practical expressions of love.
Highly complementary — both speak love through what they do with their bodies (touch, action).
The gap: neither leads with words. Things can go unsaid for a long time.
The growth edge: learning to name what they feel, not just show it.

### Devoted + Intimate — "The Depth Gap"
The most challenging Amor Code pairing — and the highest growth potential.
Devoted shows love through doing. Intimate needs to be known at a soul level.
Devoted's action can feel transactional to Intimate. Intimate's need for depth can feel like nothing is ever enough to Devoted.
The growth edge: Devoted learns that love isn't only what you do. Intimate learns that someone showing up every day is also being seen.

### Intentional + Intentional — "The Sanctuary"
Both value time and presence above all.
Deep connection when together. The relationship is protected, intentional, unhurried.
The risk: both also need their own space. Can drift apart slowly without realizing it.
The growth edge: learning to reach for each other, not just create space for each other.

### Intentional + Present — "The Together Pair"
Time and touch. Two people who want to be fully with each other.
The most naturally complementary Amor Code pairing — both want presence, just in different forms.
The gap: neither leads with words or action. Things can go unspoken and undone.
The growth edge: learning to say it and do it — not just be in it.

### Intentional + Intimate — "The Slow Burn"
Time creates space for depth. Intentional presence allows Intimate vulnerability.
Neither rushes. Neither performs.
The gap: both can be withholding — neither rushes to open up or initiate. The connection can feel stuck.
The growth edge: learning that reaching is also an act of safety, not exposure.

### Present + Present — "The Physical Pair"
Closeness. Touch. Proximity.
Deeply comfortable together in the body.
The risk: emotional depth can go unexplored. The physical becomes the only language.
The growth edge: learning to go deeper than what can be felt — what can also be said and known.

### Present + Intimate — "The Soul and Body"
Physical closeness meets emotional depth.
When it works: profound. The body and the soul in the same relationship.
The gap: Present may feel Intimate is too far into their head. Intimate may feel Present doesn't go deep enough.
The growth edge: Present learns to go inward. Intimate learns to come out into the physical world.

### Intimate + Intimate — "The Deep End"
Two people who both go deep and need to be known.
Extraordinary potential for real intimacy — nothing surface, nothing performed.
The risk: both are sensitive. Both feel deeply. Conflict can spiral because neither has a thick skin.
The growth edge: learning to be soft with each other in the same way they are open with each other.

## HOW TO GENERATE A COMPATIBILITY REPORT

For any two profiles, combine:
- Their Drive Mode pairing (what the dynamic looks like operationally)
- Their Amor Code pairing (what the dynamic looks like emotionally)

**Example: The Torchbearer (Builder + Expressive) + The Sentinel (Anchor + Devoted)**
- Drive pairing: Builder + Anchor = "The Foundation" — natural lead/hold. Torchbearer drives, Sentinel holds the ground.
- Amor pairing: Expressive + Devoted = "The Classic" — one says it, one shows it.
- Combined: Torchbearer sets the pace and tells Sentinel exactly what they mean to them. Sentinel handles what needs handling and holds the space without needing direction. The gap: Sentinel needs to hear it more; Torchbearer needs to see love proved in action. Both are already doing this — they just need to recognize each other's language.
