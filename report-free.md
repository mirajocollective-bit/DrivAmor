## DRIVE LEAD PARAGRAPH LIBRARY

### Builder
You are someone who moves. Not because you're impatient — because you understand that waiting is also a choice, and it's usually the wrong one. In the room, you are the force that shifts things from possibility to reality. In your relationship, this shows up as someone who takes on the hard calls, handles what needs handling, and refuses to let the people you love sit in problems that could be solved. The risk isn't that you push too hard. The risk is that the people around you forget you also need to be held sometimes — because you never slow down long enough to show them.

### Amplifier
You are someone who changes the temperature of every room you enter. Not by force — by presence. People feel more capable when you're near them. More seen. More possible. In your relationship, this is the gift that often goes unnamed: your partner operates at a higher level because of what you put into the air around them. The risk isn't that you give too much energy. The risk is that nobody thinks to pour back into you — because you make it look effortless, and effortless things get taken for granted.

### Anchor
You are someone who holds. Not because you can't move, but because you understand that everything needs a foundation. You are the person people don't realize they're leaning on until they step back and notice the shape of the support. In your relationship, you are consistency personified — the same person on good days and hard ones, in seasons of abundance and seasons of drought. The risk isn't that you give too much steadiness. The risk is that your stillness gets mistaken for not needing anything — when you need just as deeply as anyone else. You just don't say it.

### Architect
You are someone who sees. Not what's in front of you — what's coming. While others react to the present, you're already building the future in your head, running scenarios, accounting for what could go wrong before it does. In your relationship, this shows up as someone who plans with intention, loves with purpose, and refuses to let the relationship drift. The risk isn't that you think too much. The risk is that the people you love feel like items on a blueprint instead of the reason you're building in the first place.

## AMOR GIVE PARAGRAPH LIBRARY

### Expressive
The way you love has a voice. You don't assume people know — you tell them. Specifically, often, in the exact words that hit the way you mean them to. This is a rarer gift than you know. Most people in someone's life will never say the thing directly. You say it. The blind spot: you give in words, which means you also track love in words. When they stop coming from someone else, you notice immediately — even when they're showing up in every other way.

### Devoted
The way you love has hands. You show up. You handle things. You anticipate what's needed before anyone asks and move toward it without being told. In a world full of people who mean well and do little, you are the person who actually does. The blind spot: your love is in the doing, which means the people you love may not always see it as love — they see it as helpfulness. And you may be pouring out constantly without anyone thinking to ask what you need in return.

### Intentional
The way you love has presence. You give people your time like it's the most valuable thing you own — because it is. When you're with someone, you're actually with them. No divided attention, no half-presence. You create the kind of space where people feel like the only person in the room. The blind spot: because you give time so deliberately, you also notice when yours isn't being protected. Distraction from someone you love doesn't feel small. It lands like a statement.

### Present
The way you love has warmth. You stay close. Proximity is how you say what language can't always reach — a hand, a presence, a physical nearness that tells someone they are wanted and they are safe. The blind spot: because closeness is your language, distance is never neutral to you. When someone pulls back physically — even for ordinary reasons — you feel it before you understand it. Your body knows something is off before your mind catches up.

### Intimate
The way you love goes deep. You don't do surface. You're not interested in the curated version of someone — you want the real one. The parts they don't show easily. The history they don't lead with. The fear underneath the confidence. This is a rare and demanding kind of love, and not everyone is ready for it. The blind spot: because you go deep, you also give depth — and when someone can't or won't meet you there, it registers not just as a gap in connection but as a question about whether you're truly loved at all.
